Coated Peanut Recipe

Coated peanuts are crunchy snacks made by covering raw or roasted peanuts with a layer of seasoned flour or starch batter, then deep-frying or baking them until golden brown. The coating gives them a crispy outer shell while the inside remains nutty and flavorful.
They are popular worldwide under different names—such as peanut burger in West Africa, cracker nuts in Asia, and Japanese-style peanuts—often enjoyed as a snack or party treat.

Recipe Information

  • Preparation time: 20 minutes
  • Cooking time: 15 minutes
  • Servings: 6 - 8
  • Difficulty Level: Beginner

Ingredients

  • 2 cups raw peanuts (skinless, unsalted)
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon baking powder
  • 2 medium eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons milk (optional, for smoother coating)
  • Vegetable oil (for deep frying)

Instructions

  1. Wash and dry the raw peanuts thoroughly to remove moisture.
  2. In a bowl, beat the eggs, add sugar, salt, vanilla extract, and milk (if using), and mix well.
  3. In another bowl, combine the flour and baking powder.
  4. Add the peanuts into the egg mixture and coat them lightly.
  5. Transfer the coated peanuts into the flour mixture and toss until evenly covered.
  6. Repeat the egg and flour coating process 2 - 3 times to achieve a thick, crunchy layer.
  7. Heat vegetable oil in a deep pan over medium heat.
  8. Fry the coated peanuts in batches, stirring continuously for even browning.
  9. Remove from oil once golden brown and place on paper towels to drain excess oil.
  10. Allow to cool completely before serving or storing in an airtight container.
